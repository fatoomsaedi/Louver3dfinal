LOUVER masterpieces

The Louvre Museum in Paris is one of the world's most famous and
largest art museums, housing thousands of artworks from ancient
civilizations to the 19th century. Originally a royal palace, it became a
museum in 1793. Its collection includes iconic pieces like the Mona Lisa
and Venus de Milo. The museum's striking glass pyramid entrance,
designed by architect I. M. Pei, adds a modern touch to its historic
architecture. The Louvre remains a global cultural landmark, drawing
millions of visitors each year

The most important masterpieces
in the museum

Mona Lisa

One of the most famous and recognized paintings
globally, The Mona Lisa is celebrated for its enigmatic
smile and innovative Leonardo's use of sfumato, a
technique that creates soft transitions between light
and shadow, giving the subject a lifelike quality. Her
gaze, which seems to follow the viewer from every
angle, adds to the painting's mysterious allure.

The Code of Hammurabi

one of the earliest and most complete written
legal codes, Carved onto a large stone stele, it
consists of 282 laws that cover various aspects of
daily life, including justice, property, family, and
labor. The laws are based on the principle of lex
talionis or "an eye for an eye," prescribing
punishments that correspond to the crime.

The Winged Victory

The Statute is an ancient Greek sculpture that
represents the goddess Nike, symbolizing victory.
It s famous for its dynamic pose with Nike
appearing to descend from the heavens with her
wings spread wide, conveying a sense of
movement and triumph. Though the arms and
head are missing.
